About us

Since the day of medieval knight of St. John, the hospitaller till date, ladies and gentlemen have continued to fight for space. For the medieval knight, it was securing Jerusalem and fighting for sovereignty of Christianity, freedom of worship and advancement of God’s kingdom on earth. But today, we have encroaching into our space, corruption, immorality, secularism, and host of other vices.

To secure this space, Knights and Ladies of St. John International, an offshoot of the medieval knights of St. John the Hospitaller has preoccupied herself with promoting chivalry, charity, humility, and hospitality. Incorporated in the year 1886 by the law of the United States, the order has drawn ladies and gentlemen in 18 countries, which includes United States, Canada, the UK, Germany, Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, Trinidad and Tobago. ...

Guided by the objectives of our founding fathers, the order has continued to engage her members in works of charity in the church, catechization, contribution to vocation drive, evangelization, fraternity among members, care for women and children, fighting corruption in the society and encouragement of civil responsibility.

District 20, is one of the military units of the Lagos Grand Commandry. It is a battalion that spans across the Catholic Archdiocese of Ibadan, dioceses of Oyo, Oshogbo, Ondo, Ekiti and Ilorin. The battalion is made of 8 Commanderies - St. Patrick’s Commandery 474, Ado-Ekiti; St. Joseph’s Commandery 485, Ilorin; Sacred Heart Commandery 496, Akure; Ascension Commandery 573, Ibadan; St. Michael’s Commandery 718, Ado-Ekiti; St. Patrick’s Commandery 772, Ondo; St. Gregory’s Commandery 773, Ikare; and Sacred Heart Commandery 888, Osogbo.

The Order is a family apostolate that has units for every member of the family;

Men – Knights,
Women – Ladies Auxiliary,
Boys (Below 18 years) – Cadets, and
Girls (Below 18 years) – Juniors.

Membership

Membership is open to Catholics, above the age of 16, who are ready to further commit their time, talent and treasure to the cause of the Church. With recommendation from the Parish priest and a knight in good standing, aspirants are prepared for membership. The preparation includes, but not limited to, knowledge of Catechism of the Catholic Church, History of the Church and Order, and Code of Canon law.
